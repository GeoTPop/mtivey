GRAV

Gravity field modeling toolbox

Maurice A. Tivey : Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution


GBOX - Compute vertical gravitational attraction of a rectangular prism of density rho, at a single observation point (x0,y0,z0) 

   g=gbox(x0,y0,z0,x1,y1,z1,x2,y2,z2,density);

GBOX2D - Compute vertical gravitational attraction of a rectangular prism of density rho with limited lateral extent along a profile (an array of observation points x0,y0,z0). 

    g=gbox2d(x0,y0,z0,x1,y1,z1,x2,y2,z2,density);

GBOX3D - Compute vertical gravitational attraction of a rectangular prism of density rho for a grid  (map) of observation points x0,y0,z0. 

    g=gbox3d(x0,y0,z0,x1,y1,z1,x2,y2,z2,density);

GSPHERE - Compute all three gravity field components of a buried sphere at an observation point (x0,y0,z0). 

    [gx,gy,gz]=gsphere(x0,y0,z0,xq,yq,zq,sphere_radius,sphere_density);

GRVSPH - Compute vertical gravitational attraction of a buried sphere following Telford et al.. 

    g=grvsph(sphere_radius,sphere_depth,nx,dx,sphere_density);

GCYL - Compute vertical gravitational attraction of a buried horizontal cylinder (after Blakely, 1995). Note: this routine outputs two components of gravity: horizontal and vertical. 

    [gx,gy,gz]=grvsph(x0,z0,xq,yq,cylinder_radius,cylinder_density);

G_POLY - Compute vertical gravitational attraction of a two-dimensional body with polygonal cross-section at an observation point (x0,z0).  Body extends to infinity striking in the y-direction.  Based on Won and Bevis, Geophysics, (1988) algorithm.

    g=g_poly(xs,zs,xcorn,zcorn,density);

GPOLY2D - Compute vertical gravitational attraction of a two-dimensional body with polygonal cross-section for a profile of points (x0,z0).  Body extends to infinity in the y-direction.  Corners identified in a clockwise fashion looking along the y-axis with the x-axis to the right. 

   g=gpoly2d(x0,z0,xcorn,zcorn,ncorn,density);

GLAYER3D - Compute the vertical gravitational attraction of a two-dimensional grid of varying density confined to a plane layer. 

   g=glayer3d(density,dx,dy,z1,z2);

GSYN2D – Forward model the vertical gravitational attraction of a constant thickness layer with variable topography and density along a profile using Parker’s Fourier transform method.

   g=gsyn2d(density,topography,zobs,thickness,dx);

GINV2D - Inversion of a gravity profile for density variation within a constant thickness layer with variable topography along a profile using Parker’s Fourier transform method.

   density=ginv2d(gravity_fld,topography,wlong,wshort,zobs,thickness,dx);

GSYN3D - Forward model the vertical gravitational attraction of a constant thickness layer with variable topography and density for a gridded surface using Parker’s Fourier transform method.

   g=gsyn2d(density,topography,zobs,thickness,dx,dy);

GINV3D - Inversion of a two-dimensional grid of gravity for density assuming the source is confined to a constant thickness layer whose upper surface is defined by bathymetry.  Uses the Parker and Huestis (1974) Fourier transform approach. 

   density=ginv3d(gravity_grid,bathy_grid,wlong,wshort,observation_level,thickness,dx,dy);
